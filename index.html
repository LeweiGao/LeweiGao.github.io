I am completing a Master of Urban Innovation at the University of Toronto, building on an Honours Bachelor of Science in Geographic Information Systems, Human Geography, and Mathematics.

My work sits at the intersection of spatial analysis, urban policy, and public-sector decision-making — using maps and data to surface insights about equity, access, and how cities function for the people who live in them.

Through applied research and professional placements with the Ontario Ministry of Municipal Affairs and Housing and the City of Toronto Housing Secretariat, I have developed experience translating complex spatial and policy data into evidence-based recommendations for planning and governance. My research on food access and socioeconomic deprivation in Mississauga was published in Urban Science (2025).
